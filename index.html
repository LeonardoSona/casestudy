Case Study Assumptions

  • The Central Data Office team partners with all domains to enable Data Foundation.
  • Data quality and trust are not yet high, but teams are open to improvement ideas and discussion.
  • A minimal environment is available with: Data Fabric (metadata, policy, lineage, automation) and enables a data mesh operating model (domain ownership, federated governance), resulting in clear accountability, and possible reuse across domains.

Approach to the Challenge

From silos to shared value creation

Key Questions

“How would you align Quality Supply Chain needs with the central Data Office's vision of reusable enterprise assets?”

“How would you balance “enterprise standards” with “local function-specific requirements”?”

Proposed Solution

  • Align QSC with enterprise vision by building on standard Source and Consumer Data Products.
    • Source Data Products (Core/Foundational): Standardized, governed entities (e.g., Stock Quantity, Stock Valuation, Material Cost) with contracts, lineage, SLAs. QSC contributes domain expertise to ensure they meet real operational needs (e.g., near-real-time inventory visibility).
    • Consumer Data Products (Curated/Composable): Outcome-specific compositions from multiple Source products (e.g., Supplier Master with lead times, Stock Movement with quality flags, Cost Structure with variance tracking).
    • Cross-Domain Data Products: Products serving outcomes across domains (Inventory Optimization Engine, Working Capital Analytics) by composing Consumer and Source products across the mesh.

Implementation

  • Fabric foundation: Metadata, lineage, and policy-as-code applied to Source products; virtualization layer enables virtual consumers when materialization is not required.
  • Reusable layer: QSC owns its Consumer products while leveraging enterprise-governed Source products.
  • Product thinking: Clear ownership, discoverability, contracts, and reusability across both Source and Consumer products.

Data Product Strategy

Strategic framework for cross-domain data products

Key Questions

"How would you identify, define, and deliver the reusable data assets that QSC owns and/or needs from other functions?"

"Who should own and steward such data assets?"

Proposed Solution

  • Identify and deliver reusable data assets through structured product thinking and cross-domain collaboration.
    • QSC-Owned Assets: Apply product thinking to identify and catalog internal data products based on usage patterns and business outcomes.
    • Cross-Domain Assets: Co-create with source domains. Identify needs through business value assessment and cross-function dependency mapping. Prioritize delivery through adoption-driven roadmaps.
    • Asset Discovery: Maintain QSC Data Product Catalog showing what we own, what we consume, and usage patterns to guide future development.
  • Ownership accountability:
    • Data Council: Cross-functional body (IT, business domains, legal/compliance) for strategic decisions.
    • Platform Team: Owns technical standards, infrastructure, and cross-domain integration patterns.
    • QSC Data Stewards: Own business logic and quality standards for their area.

Implementation

  • Adoption-driven roadmaps prioritizing by business value and cross-function dependencies.
  • Collaborative development where QSC co-creates cross-domain assets with respective source domains.
  • Clear cataloging of data products, usage patterns, and ownership responsibilities.

Stakeholder Management

Building alignment across domains and leadership

Key Questions

"How would you engage other functions (Finance, Commercial, R&D) to collaborate instead of duplicating data assets?"

"How would you handle resistance (e.g., stakeholders insisting their version is different)?"

Proposed Solution

  • Engage functions through structured collaboration:
    • Quick Wins: Start with domains already experiencing pain (e.g., Finance struggling with inventory valuation inconsistencies).
    • Control Retention: Let domains keep operational control of their source systems while standardizing the shared layer.
    • Collaborative Development: Co-create shared views that serve multiple functions needs while maintaining single source of truth.
  • Validation and managed transition:
    • Acknowledge Specificity: Validate through concrete use case comparison.
    • Managed Migration: Plan systematic replacement and deprecation of QSC duplicate assets with clear timeline and parallel operation periods.
    • Change Management: Deploy tailored messaging by stakeholder based on type of change and demand, with regular progress demos and updates on planned sunset for duplicates.

Risk-Based Migration Strategy

  • Prioritization Framework:
    • High Impact, Low Risk First: Start with reference data (Supplier master, Material master).
    • Critical Path Analysis: Map dependencies, migrate upstream Source products before downstream Consumer products.
    • Business Continuity Gates: No legacy system shutdown until new shared asset meets 100% of critical use cases with equivalent or better performance.
  • Contingency Planning:
    • Rollback Strategy: Keep parallel systems for 90 days post-migration for critical assets.
    • Performance Guarantees: If shared assets miss SLAs, allow temporary local views or exemptions.
    • Stakeholder Compensation: Priority support for functions contributing high-value assets.

Adoption and Trust

Ensuring successful implementation and user confidence

Key Questions

"What steps would you take to build trust in the shared data assets?"

"What metrics would you use to measure adoption and success?"

Proposed Solution

Measure and expose key metrics:

  • Trust Metrics: Data Quality Pass Rate (97%+), Lineage Coverage (92%+), SLA Adherence (95%+), Mean Time to Resolution (<4 hours for P1 issues)
  • Adoption Journey Tracking: Monitor progression through Aware → Try → Use → Rely → Advocate stages, identifying conversion rates and drop-off points to target enablement efforts by stage.
  • Business Impact Metrics:
    • Duplicate data processing cost reduction (target: 40% by Year 1)
    • Time-to-insight improvement (target: 50% faster analytics delivery)
    • Cross-domain collaboration increase (measured by shared data product usage)
    • Data-driven decision quality (reduction in data-related business errors)
    • Regulatory compliance improvement (audit finding reduction)

Implementation:

  • Strategic Rollout: Start with high-impact, low-risk use cases to build early wins and momentum.
  • Champion Network: Identify and enable domain champions through requirements gathering, training sessions, and ongoing support to create grassroots advocacy.

Questions, feedback, or next steps?

Recap

  • Align local needs with enterprise assets via the three-layer model (Source → Consumer → Cross-Domain).
  • Make ownership explicit, and drive adoption with trust metrics and transparent engagement.
